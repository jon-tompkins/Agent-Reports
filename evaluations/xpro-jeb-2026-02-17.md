## The Business

Expro Group is an oilfield services outfit that's been around since 1938. They handle the full lifecycle of oil wells — from drilling the hole to maintaining it over decades. Think of them as the specialized mechanics for energy infrastructure across 50 countries. Not glamorous, but necessary as long as the world needs oil and gas.

## The Numbers

- **Revenue:** $1.66B (TTM, growing steadily)
- **Margins:** 4.2% net (improving but still thin)
- **FCF:** $167M levered FCF (solid cash generation)
- **Debt:** 12.5% debt/equity (practically nothing)
- **Current Price:** $16.33 | **Valuation:** 27.2x P/E, 1.14x P/S

**Market Cap:** $1.85B  
**52-Week Range:** $6.70 - $17.00 (trading near highs)

## The Moat

Modest. This isn't Coca-Cola, son. XPRO has specialized expertise in offshore and international markets, plus some proprietary tech for well testing. But it's still a services business where the lowest bid often wins. Scale matters, and they're a minnow swimming with sharks like Schlumberger and Halliburton.

**What protects them:** International focus (80% of revenue), specialized equipment, long-term service contracts. 
**What doesn't:** Limited pricing power, cyclical demand, commoditized services.

## The Risks

- **Oil price sensitivity:** When crude drops, capex gets slashed first
- **Small fish problem:** Can't muscle their way into big contracts like SLB
- **Valuation stretched:** 27x P/E for a cyclical services business? That's rich
- **Execution risk:** Recent M&A integration still ongoing

## The Verdict: **HOLD/TRIM**

- **The good news:** Oil services are having their moment. Sector's up huge while the market's flat. Venezuela reopening provides tailwinds. Balance sheet is pristine.
- **The bad news:** Stock's up 143% from its $6.70 low. At 27x earnings, you're paying for perfection in a cyclical business.
- **Fair value estimate:** $12-14 (18-20x normalized earnings)
- **Margin of safety:** -17% to -27% (stock is overvalued)

## Jeb's Color

Look, I've seen this movie before. Oil services stocks get beaten to death in the bad times, then they rocket ship when things turn. We're in the rocket ship phase now, and XPRO's had a hell of a run — up 22% just this year while the S&P's gone nowhere.

The business is decent. Management's buying back stock aggressively. International exposure is a plus. But at 27 times earnings? Son, that's what you pay for a software company, not a cyclical oil services outfit that makes 4% margins.

If you own it, take some profits. If you don't, wait for the next downturn — and there will be one. Oil always cycles, and when it does, you'll get XPRO in the single digits again.

**Grade: C+** (Decent business, wrong price)
