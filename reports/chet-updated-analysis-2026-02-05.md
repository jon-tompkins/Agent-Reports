#### 5. **FIVN - Five9 Inc.**

### 5 NEW BEARISH PICKS (Large-cap, Over $100B Market Cap)

- **Market Cap:** ~$250B
- **Theme Disruption:** Business Democratization (AI agents replacing CRM interfaces)
- **Thesis:** AI agents like Claude Cowork threatening core CRM interface monopoly. If users interact with AI directly and agents pull Salesforce data in background, core UI stickiness erodes. Already down 25% in 2026.
- **Catalyst:** Anthropic and other AI agents reducing Salesforce interface dependency

#### 2. **ADBE - Adobe**
- **Market Cap:** ~$180B
- **Theme Disruption:** AI Integration (AI replacing creative tools)
- **Thesis:** Generative AI tools (Midjourney, DALL-E, etc.) democratizing content creation. Subscription model pricing out small businesses while AI alternatives become free/cheap. Creative workflow disruption accelerating.
- **Catalyst:** AI creative tools reaching professional quality, reducing Adobe dependency

#### 3. **NOW - ServiceNow**
- **Market Cap:** ~$150B
- **Theme Disruption:** Business Democratization (AI replacing workflow automation)
- **Thesis:** Workflow automation being disrupted by AI agents that can handle complex business processes without rigid pre-built workflows. Legacy approach to business process management.
- **Catalyst:** AI agents automating business processes more flexibly than ServiceNow's rigid systems

#### 4. **WMT - Walmart**
- **Market Cap:** ~$650B
- **Theme Disruption:** IRL Premium + AI Integration
- **Thesis:** Physical retail vulnerable to AI-powered personalized commerce. While people value IRL experiences, they don't necessarily value traditional big-box shopping. AI enables hyper-personalized online experiences.
- **Catalyst:** AI-powered shopping assistants reducing need for physical store browsing

#### 5. **IBM - International Business Machines**
- **Market Cap:** ~$130B
- **Theme Disruption:** Business Democratization + AI Integration
- **Thesis:** Traditional consulting and enterprise services being democratized by AI. SMBs can now access AI-powered solutions without expensive IBM implementations. Legacy technology transition too slow.
- **Catalyst:** AI democratizing enterprise solutions that previously required IBM consultants

## KEY THEMES ANALYSIS

### 1. **AI Integration** - STRONGEST THEME
- **Winners:** INOD, AEHR, VRT, TSM, MRVL
- **Insight:** Infrastructure providers are the clear winners vs. software applications
- **Risk:** Overvaluation in smaller players, cyclicality in chip testing

### 2. **Energy Demand** - EMERGING POWERHOUSE  
- **Winners:** VRT (cooling), AEHR (chip testing), TSM (manufacturing)
- **Insight:** Data center power/cooling is the new oil rush of AI era
- **Risk:** Energy grid constraints, regulatory hurdles

### 3. **AI Onshoring** - GEOPOLITICAL TAILWIND
- **Winners:** RCAT (domestic drones), TSM (critical ally), MRVL (US-based)
- **Insight:** National security driving reshoring of critical tech
- **Risk:** Protectionist policies creating inefficiencies

### 4. **Business Democratization** - DOUBLE-EDGED
- **Winners:** FIVN (democratizing customer service)
- **Losers:** CRM, NOW, ACN, CTSH, IBM (being democratized)
- **Insight:** AI enables small businesses to compete with enterprises
- **Risk:** Disruption timeline uncertain, incumbents may adapt

### 5. **Robotics/Drone Mainstream** - EARLY STAGE
- **Winners:** RCAT, AVAV
- **Insight:** Military/defense adoption leading commercial applications
- **Risk:** Regulatory hurdles, technology not fully mature

### 6. **IRL Premium** - SELECTIVE BENEFIT
- **Winners:** LYV (live events)  
- **Losers:** WMT (commodity retail)
- **Insight:** People value curated experiences, not just physical presence
- **Risk:** Economic downturn impacts discretionary experiences
