## Executive Summary

Chevron Corporation is one of the world's largest integrated energy companies, operating across the full petroleum value chain from exploration and production to refining and retail. The company just completed a transformational year, successfully closing its **$53 billion acquisition of Hess Corporation** in July 2025 after winning a contentious arbitration battle against ExxonMobil. This deal gives Chevron a 30% stake in Guyana's prolific Stabroek Block—one of the most significant oil discoveries of the past decade.

Despite strong operational execution with record production levels (up 12% worldwide in 2025), Chevron faces headwinds from a **weakening oil price environment**. Q4 2025 earnings declined to $2.8B from $3.2B year-over-year, and full-year net income dropped 30% to $12.3B. The EIA forecasts Brent crude averaging just $56/bbl in 2026, which would pressure margins further.

**Investment Thesis:** Chevron offers a **compelling value proposition** for income-oriented investors seeking energy exposure. The 3.94% dividend yield is well-covered by free cash flow, the balance sheet is solid, and Guyana provides a multi-decade growth runway. However, the stock is trading near 52-week highs with limited near-term upside as oil prices face headwinds from oversupply.

**Rating: HOLD / ACCUMULATE ON WEAKNESS** — Quality company but near-term upside capped by oil price outlook. Target entry below $165 for better risk/reward.

## Company Overview

### What They Do
Chevron is a **global integrated energy company** operating in two primary segments:

**1. Upstream (Exploration & Production)**
- Oil and natural gas exploration, development, and production
- Key assets: Permian Basin, Gulf of Mexico, Guyana (via Hess), Eastern Mediterranean, Australia LNG, Kazakhstan
- 2025 production: 3.72 MMboe/d (million barrels of oil equivalent per day)
- Record U.S. production in 2025, up 16% YoY

**2. Downstream, Midstream & Chemicals**
- Refining, marketing, and transportation of petroleum products
- Chemicals and plastics (50% of Chevron Phillips Chemical)
- Lubricants and additives
- Retail fuel stations
- Two major chemicals expansions underway

### The Hess Acquisition (Completed July 2025)
This is the most significant development for Chevron in years:

- **Deal Size:** $53 billion all-stock transaction
- **Closed:** July 18, 2025, after winning arbitration vs. ExxonMobil
- **Key Asset:** 30% stake in Guyana's Stabroek Block
- **Partners:** ExxonMobil (45% operator), CNOOC (25%)
- **Guyana Production:** Growing rapidly, Hess earned $3.1B from Guyana in 2024

The Stabroek Block contains **11.6 billion barrels of recoverable resources** with more discoveries expected. It's one of the lowest-cost, highest-margin oil developments globally with breakeven around $25-30/bbl.
