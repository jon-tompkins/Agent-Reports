# Directed Energy Weapons Naval Thesis - Investment Analysis
**Date:** March 6, 2026  
**Report Type:** Defense Technology Investment Research  
**Focus:** Naval Directed Energy Kill Chain Analysis  

## Executive Summary

The directed energy weapons (DEW) market for naval applications represents a transformative shift in maritime defense, moving from kinetic intercepts to speed-of-light engagement at pennies per shot. The thesis centers on two key technologies:

- **Spectral Beam Combining (SBC)** - Deployed now (HELIOS 60kW on USS Preble)
- **Coherent Beam Combining (CBC)** - Future "holy grail" for long-range hard kill (50-300kW+)

**Supply Chain:** Detect (DRS radar) → Track (LMT fire control) → Power Up (DRS Energy Magazine) → Convert (ESP) → Engage (SBC now / CBC future)

**Key Investment Thesis Points:**
1. Naval platforms face severe power/space constraints requiring specialized suppliers
2. High barriers to entry due to security clearances and naval qualification requirements
3. Transition from SBC (deployed) to CBC (developmental) creates multi-year runway
4. Defense spending tailwinds from geopolitical tensions driving accelerated procurement

## Conclusion

The directed energy weapons thesis represents a multi-decade transformation in naval defense capabilities. While SBC technology is proven and deployed (HELIOS), the transition to CBC represents significant upside for specialized suppliers.

**Key takeaways:**
- **Supply chain concentration** creates moats for qualified suppliers
- **Technical barriers** limit new entrants to the market
- **Long development cycles** provide revenue visibility
- **Geopolitical tensions** accelerate adoption timelines

**Recommended action:** Begin building positions on any market weakness, with emphasis on pure plays (ESP, LASR) while maintaining defensive core (DRS, LMT).
