## The Data Center Power Thesis

### Demand Explosion
| Metric | 2025 | 2035E | Growth |
|--------|------|-------|--------|
| US Data Center Power | 41 GW | 176 GW | 4.3x |
| Global DC Electricity | ~400 TWh | ~945 TWh | 2.4x |

**Key Drivers:**
- AI model training requires massive compute
- Hyperscalers (MSFT, GOOG, AMZN, META) building capacity aggressively
- Power grid constraints limiting new data center siting
- Renewable intermittency requires reliable baseload

### Why Microreactors?
1. **Co-location:** Can deploy at data center site
2. **Reliability:** 24/7 baseload, no intermittency
3. **Carbon-free:** Meets ESG/net-zero commitments
4. **Scalability:** Modular design, add units as needed
5. **Grid independence:** Avoids transmission bottlenecks

### NNE's Data Center Partnerships

- **Digihost (Dec 2024):** 60MW power plant replacement in Upstate NY
- **BaRupOn (Nov 2025):** 1 GW feasibility study, 15+ KRONOS reactors

## Conclusion

**Rating: SPECULATIVE / SMALL POSITION ONLY**

NNE is a pure optionality play on the AI data center power thesis. The setup is compelling: exploding power demand, grid constraints, and carbon-free requirements create a real market need for microreactors.
