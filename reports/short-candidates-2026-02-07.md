## Target Analysis by Category

### Category 1: Accounting Fraud & Related Party Issues

#### **Super Micro Computer (SMCI)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Current Data:**
- **Market Cap:** $18.5B (✅ Above $5B threshold)
- **Stock Price:** ~$30.12 (down from 2024 highs)
- **P/E Ratio:** 24.4x (below 30x threshold but strong fraud case)
- **Beta:** 1.52 (high volatility)

**The Hindenburg Allegations (August 2024):**
Per Hindenburg Research's damning report titled *"Fresh Evidence Of Accounting Manipulation, Sibling Self-Dealing And Sanctions Evasion"*:

1. **Recurring Accounting Manipulation:** Despite a 2020 SEC settlement, a lawsuit filed in April 2024 alleges SMCI restarted "improper revenue recognition," "recognizing incomplete sales," and "circumvention of internal accounting controls" just 3 months after the settlement.

2. **Related Party Self-Dealing:** Evidence of undisclosed transactions with affiliated companies controlled by company insiders.

3. **Export Control Violations:** Allegations of sanctions evasion and export control failures.

4. **Customer Concentration Risk:** Over-reliance on specific customers with questionable relationship dynamics.

**Stock Impact:** Shares plummeted 40%+ after the report, but remain elevated from pre-AI boom levels.

**Short Thesis:** Strong accounting fraud case with regulatory overhang, high AI-driven valuation vulnerable to continued revelations.

### Category 2: Bad Loan Exposure & Subprime Risk

#### **Upstart Holdings (UPST)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Current Data:**
- Estimated market cap: $2-4B range (below preferred $5B threshold)
- Stock down significantly from AI-boom highs
- Recent issues: Stock plummeted 14.8% in November 2025

**Dual Risk Profile:**
1. **Subprime Lending Exposure:** Core business is AI-powered personal lending to non-prime borrowers
2. **AI Model Failure:** Company's own AI model has been "overreacting" to macro signals, causing it to tighten credit standards and miss loan volume targets

**The Perfect Storm:**
- Rising credit losses in subprime segment
- AI model producing false signals 
- Reduced loan origination volumes
- Fintech lending sector under pressure

**Quote:** "Upstart's stock plummeted 14.8% not because of poor profits, but because its own AI model intentionally 'tightened the credit box,' causing a miss on loan origination volume."

**Short Thesis:** Company caught between deteriorating credit environment and malfunctioning core AI technology.

---

#### **Affirm Holdings (AFRM)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Current Data:**
- Recent price: ~$44 (down from $80.98 February 2025 high)
- Processing $37B in transactions with 24M users
- Market position: Largest U.S. BNPL provider

**Credit Risk Explosion:**
Per Consumer Financial Protection Bureau and industry reports:
- **65% of BNPL loans** go to borrowers with risky credit scores
- Klarna (competitor) saw credit losses rise 17% in 2025
- BNPL industry facing "growing debt crisis" per analysts
- Many borrowers hold multiple BNPL accounts across platforms

**Operational Concerns:**
- Rising loan loss provisions anticipated
- Economic slowdown would disproportionately impact subprime BNPL users
- Regulatory scrutiny increasing on BNPL practices

**Short Thesis:** Positioned at the epicenter of consumer credit deterioration with business model dependent on continued subprime lending growth.

## Investment Thesis & Strategy

### Primary Short Candidates

**1. Super Micro Computer (SMCI)** - Strongest Case
- **Catalyst:** Ongoing fraud investigation, potential SEC action
- **Risk:** High beta, can squeeze violently
- **Strategy:** Wait for negative earnings or regulatory news

**2. Upstart Holdings (UPST)** - Credit + AI Double Whammy  
- **Catalyst:** Continued credit deterioration, AI model failures
- **Risk:** Already down significantly from highs
- **Strategy:** Monitor credit trends and loan performance

**3. Affirm Holdings (AFRM)** - Subprime BNPL Implosion
- **Catalyst:** Rising delinquencies, regulatory crackdown
- **Risk:** Strong market position provides some protection
- **Strategy:** Watch for credit loss provisions increases

### Risk Management

**Position Sizing:** Start small - these names can be volatile
**Hedging:** Consider put spreads vs outright shorts on high-short-interest names
**Timing:** Wait for specific catalysts rather than thesis-based entries
**Diversification:** Spread across different risk categories (fraud vs credit vs AI)

### Key Metrics to Monitor

**For Credit Plays (UPST, AFRM, SOFI):**
- 60+ day delinquency rates
- Loan loss provision trends  
- Consumer spending/employment data
- Interest rate environment changes

**For Fraud Plays (SMCI):**
- SEC investigation updates
- Hindenburg follow-up reports
- Auditor opinions/delays
- Management guidance changes

**For AI Disruption:**
- User/subscriber trends
- Revenue per user metrics
- Competitive AI tool adoption
- Management commentary on AI impact
