## Executive Summary

Super Micro Computer is a Silicon Valley-based server manufacturer that rode the AI wave to spectacular gains before a cascade of governance failures brought the stock crashing back to earth. The company was **previously delisted in 2018** for accounting violations, settled with the SEC for $17.5 million in 2020, and has now faced a second round of similar allegations from Hindenburg Research. Ernst & Young resigned as auditor in October 2024 citing "integrity" concerns—an extraordinarily rare and damaging move. While SMCI narrowly avoided delisting by filing delinquent financials in February 2025, **material weaknesses in internal controls remain unremediated**.

**Thesis Fit:** ⚠️ **HIGH-RISK SHORT CANDIDATE** — Serial recidivist with repeated accounting issues, but timing is difficult given AI demand tailwinds.

## Company Overview

### What They Do
Super Micro Computer is a "Rack-Scale Total IT Solutions provider" offering:
- **AI/GPU Servers:** Training and inferencing systems with integrated Nvidia GPUs
- **Storage Systems:** Enterprise data storage solutions
- **Direct Liquid Cooling (DLC):** Technology for high-density AI server cooling
- **Edge/5G Systems:** Embedded solutions for telecommunications

The company was founded in 1993 by CEO Charles Liang and his wife Sara Liu (an accountant). It went public via $64M IPO in 2007.

### Business Model
- **Revenue Mix:** 92% from servers/storage, remainder from subsystems and accessories
- **Geographic:** 61% US, 24% Asia, 10% Europe
- **Customers:** OEM/Large Data Centers (64%), Enterprise/Channel (34%)
- **Key Partners:** Nvidia (critical GPU supplier), CoreWeave, Tesla (historically)

### The AI Server Boom
SMCI was an early mover in AI infrastructure, gaining massive market share as demand for GPU-dense servers exploded:
- Market cap surged from **$2.2B (end 2021) to $67B peak (March 2024)**
- Added to S&P 500 in March 2024, Nasdaq 100 in July 2024
- Revenue growth of **+46.6% YoY in FY2025** to $21.97B

## The Hindenburg Allegations (August 2024)

On August 27, 2024, Hindenburg Research published a devastating short report. Key allegations:

### 1. Accounting Manipulation (Again)
- SMCI was delisted in 2018 for failing to file financials
- Settled with SEC in 2020 for **$17.5M** over "widespread accounting violations"
- Violations included: **$200M+ in improperly recognized revenue**, understated expenses
- According to a **2024 whistleblower lawsuit**, SMCI resumed improper practices **within 3 months** of the SEC settlement

### 2. Rehired Scandal-Linked Executives
- Three senior employees who left during the 2018 scandal were later rehired:
  - One as **board member**
  - One as **consultant to CEO**
  - One as **VP of Business Development**
- Former CFO Howard Hideshima (individually charged by SEC) was hired by a related party in 2023

### 3. Related Party Self-Dealing
- **Ablecom & Compuware:** Companies run by CEO's brothers paid $983M over 3 years
- CEO Charles Liang and wife **partially own Ablecom**
- ~99.8% of Ablecom's US exports go to Super Micro
- ~99.7% of Compuware's US exports go to Super Micro
- Circular relationships: SMCI provides components → brothers assemble → sell back to SMCI

### 4. Undisclosed Related Parties
- Additional brothers own Taiwan entities supplying SMCI (not disclosed)
- Entities operate out of "Super Micro Science and Technology Park" in Taiwan
- **Lambda Labs investment:** Undisclosed investment as part of $320M round
- Signed "unusual" $600M datacenter sublease with Lambda

### 5. Sanctions Evasion
- 2006: Pleaded **guilty to felony** for exporting banned components to Iran
- Since Russia invasion (Feb 2022): Exports to Russia spiked **~3x**
- At least 46 companies handling SMCI products to Russia now under OFAC sanctions
- **$46.3M** in products shipped to Russian "supercomputer" supplier

### 6. Quality & Customer Issues
- **Digital Ocean:** Switched to Dell after "train wreck" reliability issues
- **Genesis Cloud:** Called it "catastrophic" and "worst experience in industry"
- **GMI Cloud:** 17.5% malfunction rate, moving to HPE

- **Tesla/xAI:** Dell has won major deals, eroding SMCI exclusivity
- Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang publicly praised **Dell** as better at enterprise systems
